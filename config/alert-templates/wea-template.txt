WIRELESS EMERGENCY ALERT (WEA) TEMPLATE
========================================
MAXIMUM 360 CHARACTERS — plain language, no abbreviations that could confuse.

Format:
[EVENT_TYPE] [ALERT_TIER] for [ZONE]. [ONE_PROTECTIVE_ACTION]. [ONE_SENTENCE_THREAT_DESCRIPTION]. [OFFICIAL_INFO_SOURCE].

Examples (use as reference, draft fresh for each event):

FLOOD WARNING: "Flash Flood Warning for North Valley. Move to higher ground NOW. 2-4 inches of rain expected in next hour causing life-threatening flooding. For info: county.gov/emergency or call 555-911-0100"
(178 chars)

WILDFIRE: "Evacuation Order for Hillside District zones A-C. Leave immediately via Hwy 101 North. Wildfire 2 miles away, wind-driven. Shelter: Central High School, 1100 Education Blvd. Info: 555-911-0100"
(196 chars)

EARTHQUAKE: "Earthquake Shaking Alert. Drop, Cover, Hold On. 6.2 magnitude near Downtown. Aftershocks expected. Avoid damaged buildings. If smell gas, evacuate. Info: 555-911-0100"
(168 chars)

HURRICANE: "Hurricane Warning for all zones. Evacuate coastal areas NOW via posted routes. Category 3, landfall in 12 hours. Winds 120mph, life-threatening storm surge. Shelters open at Convention Center. 555-911-0100"
(207 chars)

NOTES:
- Keep under 360 characters total
- Lead with event type and severity
- Include ONE clear protective action
- Include information hotline
- Never use all-caps unless required (it reads as shouting in tests but is standard for EAS)
